How the Finance Bill 2023 becomes law

  • Nahashon Muiru
  • Jun 2023

We've been hearing alot about the Finance Bill which was published on 28 April 2023 and tabled before the National Assembly on 4 May 2023. It has been rocked by critics and its defendants but do we know how exactly a proposed bill becomes law? Well here's how. It transitions through the following stages

"The Bill is presented as a matter of ordinary fiscal budgetary course and would amend various tax laws, including the: Income Tax Act (ITA), VAT Act, 2013 (VAT Act), Excise Duty Act, Tax Procedures Act, 2015 (TPA), and Miscellaneous Fees and Levies Act. The Bill also provides for amendments to other non-tax statutes." ~ Ernst & Young.

1st Stage (First reading)

The bill is just simply read out and members are given time to go and look at it before the actual debate.

2nd Stage (Second reading)

For a second time the bill is read and an indepth debate carried out. Proposals are made on how to possibly improve it and a vote taken. If most of the members vote in support of it, it advances to the next stage.

3rd Stage (Committee stage)

Parliament selects a committee or transforms itself to one to look into the bill and incorporate recommendations & proposals made in the second stage. The following may happen during this stage;

  • [i]. The bill maybe rewritten
  • [ii]. The speaker may leave his/her chair.
  • [iii]. Standing orders are relaxed to allow members to freely discuss the bill. It cannot die at this stage as there is no voting yet.

4th stage (Report Stage)

The Chairman of the committee reports back to the house on their findings and a vote is taken.

5th stage (Third reading)

The bill is read for a third and a final time. A final debate and vote is taken allowing the bill to move to the next and final stage if voted in favour of.

6th Stage (Presidential Assent)

This is the law making process where the President signs the bill into law. The bill cannot become law until this happens.
